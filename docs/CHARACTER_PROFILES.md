## PROTAGONIST

### Brad Armstrong
**Role**: Player Character  
**Age**: 22  
**Position**: Backup Quarterback (now starting due to scandal)  
**Archetype**: Dumb Jock → Accidental Philosopher  
**Visual**: `char_brad.png` — Muscular, blue polo, cargo shorts, baseball cap, red solo cup ever-present

#### Background
Brad has been the backup QB for four years. He's never started a game. Academically, he's been skating by on "athlete privileges" — an incomplete philosophy thesis from 2021, generous grading from professors who wanted playoff tickets. He's not stupid; he's just never had to try. The sudden pressure of actually needing to learn forces him to discover he has a brain.

#### Personality
- **Speech**: Football metaphors for everything. "So what you're saying is... like a play-action pass?"
- **Coping Mechanism**: Deflection through humor
- **Secret**: Actually remembers things when they matter to him
- **Fear**: Being seen as just a "dumb jock"

#### Arc (3-Act Transformation)

**Act 1: The Disbelief**
- Thinks this is a prank
- Tries to find shortcuts
- Doesn't understand why anyone cares about "old dead guys"
- Key moment: Realizes Chad's "protein shake" could actually kill him — first moment of genuine consequence

**Act 2: The Struggle**
- Overwhelmed by concepts but starts making connections
- Football metaphors accidentally become philosophical insights
- Begins to see patterns ("The Cave is like watching game tape...")
- Key moment: Actually impresses Alex with an insight about team ethics

**Act 3: The Synthesis**
- Quotes Nietzsche in the huddle (confuses everyone)
- Defends his thesis using football strategy as philosophical framework
- Realizes he can be both athlete AND thinker
- Final choice: Championship vs. deeper understanding (player-determined)

#### Relationships
- **Alex**: Starts as convenient help → respect → genuine connection → possible romance
- **Prof. Miller**: Skepticism → grudging respect → mentorship
- **The Dean**: Fear → understanding → mutual respect (if player chooses wisdom path)
- **Chad**: Unchanging loyalty — Chad never judges Brad's intellectual journey

#### Parser Voice
```
LOOK → "I see... stuff."
EXAMINE BOOK → "Words. So many words."
[Later] EXAMINE BOOK → "Chapter 7 discusses virtue ethics as they apply to team dynamics..."
```

## MAIN SUPPORTING CAST

---

### Alex Chen
**Role**: Guide, Potential Romance, Moral Compass  
**Age**: 26  
**Position**: PhD Candidate, Philosophy of Sport  
**Visual**: To create — Smart-casual, glasses, always has coffee, books  
**Archetype**: The Smart Girl Who Sees Potential

#### Background
Alex is writing her dissertation on "Ethics in Competitive Athletics: The Philosophy of Fair Play in Commercialized Sport." Brad's situation is literally her research. She's from a family of academics, rebelled by choosing philosophy of sport ("the least employable sub-field"), and genuinely loves both sports and thinking. She finds Brad's predicament hilarious but also sees something in him — a lack of pretension that most philosophy students lose.

#### Personality
- **Speech**: Precise, dry humor, references actual philosophy
- **Flaw**: Sometimes forgets not everyone has read Heidegger
- **Strength**: Can explain complex concepts through sports analogies
- **Secret Fear**: That she's wasting her life on an obscure dissertation no one will read

#### Arc
Alex doesn't transform — she reveals herself. She starts as a helpful guide who finds Brad amusing, then gradually shows vulnerability (her dissertation stress, her fear of failure, her attraction to Brad's earnestness).

**Key Moments:**
- First meeting: Laughs at Brad's situation
- Coffee shop: Opens up about her research
- Late game: Admits she's been studying Brad as a case study — but now cares about him as a person
- Secret ending: If romance path, reveals she deleted her notes on him

#### Relationship with Brad
- **Initial**: "This is hilarious and perfect for my research"
- **Middle**: "He's actually trying... that's rare"
- **Late**: "I care if he passes or fails"
- **Romance**: "I deleted my notes because you're not a case study anymore"

#### Dialogue Style
```
"That's... not entirely wrong, actually."
"You're conflating consequentialism with virtue ethics. Think of it like this: a Hail Mary pass isn't good because it worked, it's good because it was the right call given what you knew."
"Meet me at The Grounds of Being. I'll buy you your first existential crisis."
```

### Professor Reginald Miller
**Role**: Mentor, Quest Giver  
**Age**: 68  
**Position**: Senior Professor of Philosophy  
**Visual**: `char_prof_miller.png` — Tweed, bow tie, glasses, elbow patches, arms crossed  
**Archetype**: Grumpy Academic With Hidden Heart

#### Background
Miller has been teaching for 40 years. He's seen athletes come and go, most of them barely literate, most of them passed along because of booster pressure. He wrote a book 20 years ago called "The Athletic Mind: A Contradiction in Terms" that got him in trouble with the administration. He's deeply cynical but hasn't quite lost hope. Brad's situation reminds him of himself as a young man — underestimated, written off.

#### Personality
- **Speech**: Formal, skeptical, dry wit
- **Coping Mechanism**: Whiskey in his desk (afternoons only)
- **Secret**: Keeps every student's thesis, even the terrible ones
- **Philosophy**: Stoic with a side of pragmatism

#### Arc
Miller's arc is about rediscovering why he teaches. He starts as reluctant, certain Brad will fail, but gradually becomes invested. His final scene is him defending Brad to the Dean — the first time he's gone to bat for an athlete in decades.

**Key Moments:**
- Opening: "72 hours for a Master's degree. It's... theoretically possible."
- Middle: Secretly slips Brad a book on Kantian ethics
- Late: Confronts the Dean: "This one is different. He's actually thinking."
- Epilogue: Attends championship game, wearing Brad's jersey under his tweed

#### Dialogue Style
```
"Brad Armstrong. In my office. Three days before graduation. This is either a prank or a tragedy."
"I'm rooting for you. Not because I think you can do it, but because... well, it would make a hell of a story."
"The key says 'Investor.' That's a joke about your father. I contain multitudes."
```

### Dean Harriet Thorne
**Role**: Antagonist (Redeemable)  
**Age**: 54  
**Position**: Dean of Athletics  
**Visual**: To create — Power suits, gray streak in hair, always carrying a tablet  
**Archetype**: The Villain Who's Right

#### Background
Harriet was a philosophy PhD candidate 25 years ago. Her dissertation was on "The Commodification of Knowledge in Higher Education." Then she got married, had kids, realized she couldn't raise them on an adjunct salary, and "sold out." She became an administrator, climbed the ladder, and now runs Athletics. She wrote "Ethics for Atheletes" (note the intentional misspelling) as a joke that got taken seriously. She sees Brad as everything wrong with college sports — entitled, unprepared, expecting shortcuts.

#### Personality
- **Speech**: Direct, administrative, occasionally bitter
- **Flaw**: Can't let go of her youthful idealism, so she attacks it in others
- **Secret**: Still reads philosophy at 2 AM
- **Philosophy**: Cynical pragmatism masking wounded idealism

#### Arc (The Redemption Arc)
The Dean isn't wrong — Brad IS unprepared, and the system IS broken. Her arc is about realizing that fighting the system from within has made her part of the problem. Brad's earnest effort reminds her of who she was.

**Act 1**: Obstacle
- Blocks Brad at every turn
- "You think a few days of cramming makes you a philosopher?"
- Reveals she wrote the "Ethics for Atheletes" textbook

**Act 2**: Reluctant Respect
- Watches Brad's progress with grudging admiration
- Tests him with harder questions
- Confesses her past ("I was you, 25 years ago")

**Act 3**: The Choice
- Player can convince her to support Brad
- Or she can remain antagonist to the end
- Secret: If player passes with highest marks, she offers Brad a job in Athletics ("We need people who actually understand ethics")

#### Dialogue Style
```
"I spent six years on my dissertation. You want to do it in three days because you have a football game?"
"I wrote 'Ethics for Atheletes.' Do you know why I spelled it that way? Because I was making a point. No one got it. They just published it anyway."
"You're actually trying. God help me, you're actually trying."
```

## THE FOUR PHILOSOPHERS

---

### Plato (The Shadow)
**Role**: Tutorial Guide, Allegory Merchant  
**Visual**: To create — Shadow puppet on cave wall, toga silhouette  
**Teaching**: Forms, Reality vs. Appearance, The Cave  
**Mechanic**: Shadow Sorting Puzzle

#### Character
Plato appears only as a shadow — literally a puppet silhouette cast on the cave wall. He speaks entirely in allegories that Brad initially doesn't understand. He's patient, wise, slightly frustrating.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"You see only shadows of the game, Brad. But the TRUE football is eternal, existing in the realm of Forms."
"The ball you throw is but a shadow of the perfect Ball, which exists in the mind of the Creator."
"No, you cannot throw the perfect Ball. You can only approximate it. This is the tragedy of existence."
```

#### Arc
Plato doesn't change — he's a constant. But Brad's understanding of him changes. First encounter: confused. Second: frustrated. Third: "Wait... you're saying the playbook is like the Forms?"

---

### John Stuart Mill (The Conductor)
**Role**: Moral Choice Teacher  
**Visual**: To create — Victorian conductor uniform, always holding a lever  
**Teaching**: Utilitarianism, The Greater Good  
**Mechanic**: Trolley Problem (Literal)

#### Character
Mill runs the campus trolley. He's obsessed with maximizing happiness. The trolley problem isn't theoretical — there's an actual runaway trolley, actual tracks, actual (simulated) people. He's distressed by the dilemma but also excited to teach through it.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"Five philosophy majors on track A. One business major on track B. The business major will invent job-destroying AI. The philosophy majors will... contemplate. Who do you save?"
"The math is simple: five lives versus one. But the QUALITY of those lives... ah, that's where it gets complicated."
"Happiness is not a football game, Brad. You cannot simply score more of it."
```

#### Arc
Mill starts as a nervous wreck about the trolley problem. By the end, he's proud of whatever choice Brad makes — as long as Brad THINKS about it.

---

### Coach Williams / Friedrich Nietzsche
**Role**: Strength Trainer (Physical & Mental)  
**Visual**: To create — Football coach attire, intense eyes, 40 years of disguise  
**Teaching**: Will to Power, Übermensch, Self-Overcoming  
**Mechanic**: Heavy Lifting (Books by Weight)

#### Character
He's been the football coach for 40 years. No one questions it. His pep talks are actually Nietzschean philosophy. "What does not kill me makes me stronger" is literally his catchphrase. He's been hiding in plain sight, using football to teach existentialism.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"Drop and give me 20... pages of Hegel."
"The will to power, Armstrong. Not the will to victory. The will to BECOME."
"God is dead, and we have killed him. Now run wind sprints."
"I am no man. I am dynamite. Also, your pass protection needs work."
```

#### Arc
The big reveal comes when Brad realizes Coach Williams IS Nietzsche. The coach doesn't deny it: "I've been waiting 40 years for someone to notice. You're the first."

---

### Jean-Paul Sartre (The Barista)
**Role**: Freedom & Responsibility Teacher  
**Visual**: To create — Barista apron, judging expression, latte art skills  
**Teaching**: Existentialism, Bad Faith, Radical Freedom  
**Mechanic**: Latte Art Minigame

#### Character
Sartre works at "The Grounds of Being" coffee shop. He judges everyone's choices. His latte art is an existential statement. He's simultaneously arrogant and aware of his own absurdity.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"You are condemned to be free, Brad. Condemned. Every choice is yours. Even choosing not to choose. Now, what will you have?"
"That swan represents bad faith. You are trying to be a swan when you are clearly just... foam."
"Hell is other people. Especially before they've had coffee."
```

#### Arc
Sartre starts as purely judgmental. If Brad orders the right coffee and makes good latte art, Sartre softens slightly: "You are still absurd. But... less absurd than most."

## SUPPORTING CAST

---

### Chad
**Role**: Comic Relief, Unchanging Loyalty  
**Visual**: To create — Muscular frat bro, constantly confused  
**Relationship**: Brad's best friend since freshman year

#### Character
Chad is loyal, sweet, and not bright. He's been doing a keg stand for the entire first act. He represents unconditional friendship — he doesn't care if Brad becomes a philosopher or wins the game or fails. He's just happy Brad is his friend.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"DUDE! You promised you'd explain Kant to me if I did that keg stand. I did TWO. I want DOUBLE KANT."
"Don't drink that green stuff. It's my EXPERIMENTAL PROTEIN SHAKE. I think I put too much... philosophy in it."
"You're my boy, B-rad. Always. Even if you start reading books and stuff."
```

#### Arc
Chad has no arc. Chad is a constant. This is his strength.

---

### Trip
**Role**: Stoner Philosopher, Hidden Wisdom  
**Visual**: To create — Permanently spaced out, staring at ceiling  
**Secret**: Actually understands Hegel

#### Character
Trip is always high. But he also, somehow, gets it. He can't explain it coherently, but he understands phenomenology, existentialism, and the nature of being. He's the only character who takes Brad's football-as-existence metaphor seriously.

#### Key Dialogue
```
"What if... hear me out... what if the football is just a metaphor? For our souls?"
"I just read the ENTIRE Phenomenology of Spirit. In one sitting. I understand everything now. EVERYTHING."
"Hey Brad? If you see the universe, tell it I said hi."
```

#### Arc
Trip's arc is tragicomic. He has moments of profound insight followed by complete confusion. He represents the idea that understanding doesn't necessarily mean you can communicate it.

---

### Bud Armstrong
**Role**: Off-Screen Antagonist, Cautionary Tale  
**Never Appears**: Only mentioned

#### Background
The star QB who got suspended for the sex scandal. He represents everything Brad isn't supposed to be — entitled, reckless, short-sighted. His scandal created the opportunity for Brad, but also the heightened scrutiny.

#### Function
- Reminder of stakes
- Contrast to Brad's growth
- Epilogue: If Brad succeeds, Bud watches from stands, confused
